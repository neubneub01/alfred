# ASC 740 — Income Taxes Overview

## Core Concepts

ASC 740 (formerly SFAS 109) governs accounting for income taxes under US GAAP.

### Key Principles
1. **Current tax** = taxes payable/refundable for the current year
2. **Deferred tax** = future tax consequences of temporary differences
3. **Temporary differences** = differences between tax basis and book basis of assets/liabilities
4. **Valuation allowance** = reduces deferred tax assets when "more likely than not" they won't be realized

### The Provision Process
1. Compute pretax book income (from financial statements)
2. Identify permanent differences (add back / subtract)
3. Compute current tax expense (taxable income × statutory rate)
4. Identify temporary differences
5. Compute deferred tax expense (change in deferred tax assets/liabilities)
6. Total tax expense = current + deferred
7. Compute effective tax rate (ETR) = total tax expense / pretax book income

## Common Temporary Differences

### Deferred Tax Assets (DTAs)
- Accrued liabilities (bonus, vacation, warranty)
- Bad debt reserves
- Net operating loss (NOL) carryforwards
- Tax credit carryforwards
- Lease liabilities (ASC 842)
- Stock compensation (excess book over tax)
- Inventory reserves

### Deferred Tax Liabilities (DTLs)
- Depreciation (accelerated tax vs. straight-line book)
- Prepaid expenses (cash basis tax, accrual book)
- Right-of-use assets (ASC 842)
- Goodwill (tax-deductible vs. non-amortizable book)
- Unrealized gains on investments
- Installment sales

## Valuation Allowance (ASC 740-10-30)

Record a VA when it is "more likely than not" (>50%) that some or all DTAs will not be realized.

### Positive Evidence (supports realization)
- Existing taxable temporary differences reversing
- Future taxable income (exclusive of reversing differences)
- Tax planning strategies
- History of profitability

### Negative Evidence (supports VA)
- Cumulative losses in recent years
- History of NOL/credit carryforwards expiring
- Losses expected in early future years
- Unsettled circumstances that could adversely affect future operations

## Uncertain Tax Positions (ASC 740-10-25)

### Two-Step Process
1. **Recognition**: Is it "more likely than not" the position will be sustained?
2. **Measurement**: If yes, measure at largest amount with >50% likelihood of being realized

### Disclosure Requirements
- Roll-forward of unrecognized tax benefits
- Interest and penalties policy
- Positions that could change significantly in 12 months

## Rate Reconciliation

Reconcile statutory rate to effective rate:
- Federal statutory rate: 21%
- +/- State taxes (net of federal benefit)
- +/- Permanent differences (meals, fines, tax-exempt income)
- +/- Tax credits (R&D, foreign tax)
- +/- Valuation allowance changes
- +/- Rate changes (remeasurement)
- +/- Return-to-provision adjustments
- = Effective tax rate

## Interim Reporting (ASC 740-270)

- Use estimated annual effective tax rate (EAETR)
- Discrete items recorded in quarter incurred
- Update EAETR each quarter with best available information
